sentence for contempt of court disqualified him. <!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">Mr Zuma was convicted in 2021 for refusing to testify at an inquiry investigating corruption during his presidency which ended in 2018. <!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">He has been campaigning under the banner of the newly formed uMkhonto weSizwe (MK) party after falling out with the It [the ruling] doesn&#x27;t affect our campaign at all,&quot; he said.<!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">South Africans vote for political parties, with the candidates at the top of their lists getting parliamentary seats depending on the number of votes the party gets.<!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">The electoral commission said Mr Zuma&#x27;s name would now be removed from MK&#x27;s list of parliamentary candidates, while confirming that his image would remain on ballot papers, alongside his party&#x27;s logo.  <!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">MK members sang and danced outside the court portraying Mr Zuma as a victim, while those inside  - some dressed in traditional Zulu regalia - sat silently as Justice Leona Theron read out the unanimous judgement.<!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">Mr Zuma has not yet commented on the ruling. <!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">His supporters rioted after he was sent to jail in 2021, and some of its leaders had threatened violence if the court disqualified him from standing for parliament. <!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">But MK officials have since changed their rhetoric, saying the party&#x27;s focus was on getting a two-thirds majority so that South Africa&#x27;s constitution could be changed, and Mr Zuma could be returned to power. <!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">In court, his lawyers had argued that because he was released after three months in prison by his successor, President Cyril Ramaphosa, the rest of his sentence was cancelled. <!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">But the court disagreed, saying the length of time he actually spent in prison was irrelevant.<!-- --></p><p class="sc-eb7bd5f6-0 fYAfXe">South Africa&#x27;s constitution barred anyone sentenced to 12 months in prison, without the option of a fine, from serving in parliament in order to protect the integrity of the &quot;democratic regime&quot;
