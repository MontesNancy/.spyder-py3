<p>Liya Zaurbekova, 19, fled her family home on 13 May and told Russian police she feared for her life, saying her family physically and psychologically abused her. Her father, Beslan, has denied her claims, and said the family hoped to find Aliya and bring her home. </p>
<p>In the interview, Zaurbekov blamed his daughter’s recent actions on a partner who had “misled” her and warned that her partner would be punished according to the family’s “own laws” if he did not convert to Islam.</p>
<p>Zaurbekova was detained by police and brought into a Moscow police station on Friday following a false missing minor report being filed by her family. Some 10 family members subsequently turned up at the police station to take custody of her. </p>
